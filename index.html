Food Waste Reduction System

A comprehensive digital solution to reduce food waste
at the household and community level

Food waste is one of the most pressing global challenges, with 40% of food produced in the United States being thrown away. Our team is developing an integrated mobile system that addresses food waste through smart inventory tracking, meal planning, community sharing, and educational features.

By combining automated food tracking, recipe suggestions based on available ingredients, and community sharing capabilities, our system aims to help households reduce their food waste while building more sustainable consumption habits.

Key Features

Our solution addresses food waste through multiple integrated approaches, targeting both individual and community-level challenges.

Smart Inventory Tracking

Automated food tracking with barcode scanning and expiration date monitoring to prevent spoilage and reduce waste.

Recipe Suggestions

Generate personalized meal ideas using ingredients you already have, helping you use food before it expires.

Community Sharing

Connect with neighbors and local organizations to share surplus food and reduce community-wide waste.

Education & Tips

Learn proper food storage techniques and waste reduction strategies through interactive educational content.

Research & Impact

Our solution is grounded in extensive research on food waste patterns and user behavior.

Key Statistics

  • 40% of food produced in the US is wasted - USDA findings
  • 257 pounds per household annually - Average food waste per American family
  • 1 in 8 Americans are food insecure - Making food waste reduction crucial
  • 78% of people want to reduce waste - Survey data shows motivation exists

Our Approach

Based on stakeholder analysis and task analysis, we identified key user groups including college students, busy families, and low-income households. Our solution addresses five core task categories:

  • Planning and Shopping: Smart grocery lists and meal planning
  • Inventory Management: Tracking and monitoring food items
  • Community Sharing: Connecting users to share surplus food
  • Education: Teaching storage techniques and waste reduction
  • Distribution: Facilitating producer-to-consumer connections

Success Criteria

Our project success will be measured through design validation, user experience metrics, problem-solution alignment, and educational impact. We aim for 80% user navigation success, 3.5+ satisfaction ratings, and 60% improvement in user confidence about food management.
